> ### Types of Bears in New Jersey

> ### Bear Behavior
> - **Curious**: Bears are naturally curious creatures and may approach human-occupied areas to investigate smells or objects.
> - **Defensive**: If they feel threatened, especially when cubs are involved, they can become defensive.
> - **Foraging**: In the fall, bears are in hyperphagia, meaning they're eating a lot to prepare for hibernation. This makes them more likely to venture near human-occupied areas in search of food.
> 
> ### What To Do If You Encounter a Bear
> 
> #### Stop, Speak, and Stand
> 1. **Stop**: Do not run. Running can trigger a chase response.
> 2. **Speak**: Speak in a calm, assertive voice. Let the bear know you're human and not prey.
> 3. **Stand**: Stand your ground. Make yourself look bigger by raising your arms or standing on a rock. Slowly wave your arms up and down.
> 
> #### Additional Tips
> - **Avoid Eye Contact**: Bears may perceive this as a threat.
> - **Slow Movements**: Make slow movements and avoid sudden gestures.
> - **Back Away**: Slowly back away if the bear is not approaching. Do not turn your back on the bear.
> - **Do Not Climb**: Bears are excellent climbers.
>  
> #### If a Bear Approaches or Attacks
> - **Use Bear Spray**: If you have it, use bear spray when the bear is within 40 feet.
> - **Fight Back**: As a last resort, if physical contact is made, fight back using any object available. Aim for the bear's nose and eyes.
> 
> ### Preventative Measures
> - **Make Noise**: Use bear bells or make other noises to alert bears of your presence.
> - **Travel in Groups**: Bears are less likely to approach groups.
> - **Keep a Clean Camp**: Store food and waste properly to avoid attracting bears.

> ### What Makes a Container Bear-Resistant?
> 
> A bear-resistant container is specifically designed to be difficult for bears to open. Here are some key features:
> 
> 1. **Material**: Usually made of a hard, durable material like high-strength polymer or metal that bears can't easily break or puncture.
>  
> 2. **Locking Mechanisms**: Equipped with complex locks that require opposable thumbs and a certain level of dexterity to open. Some use screw-top lids, while others have button or lever-activated locking systems.
>  
> 3. **Seals**: Often have rubber or silicone seals to lock in odors, making the containers less attractive to bears in the first place.
>  
> 4. **Certification**: Many bear-resistant containers are certified by the Interagency Grizzly Bear Committee (IGBC) or other wildlife agencies, ensuring they meet specific bear-resistant criteria.
> 
> 5. **Size and Shape**: Designed to be too large for a bear to get its jaws around but still manageable for humans to carry.
> 
> ### Is Uncooked Rice a Good Food to Have?
> 
> Uncooked rice is a compact, non-perishable food item, making it a practical choice for long-term storage. However, there are some considerations when it comes to bears:
> 
> 1. **Odor**: Uncooked rice has a minimal scent compared to other foods, making it less likely to attract bears. However, once cooked, it can emit an odor that may attract wildlife.
> 
> 2. **Storage**: If stored in a bear-resistant container, the risk of attracting bears is minimized.
> 
> 3. **Cooking**: Cooking rice will require a heat source, and the smell of cooking food can attract bears. Make sure to cook at least 100 yards away from your sleeping area and store any leftovers in a bear-resistant container.
> 
> 4. **Water**: Rice requires water to cook, so you'll need to plan your water supply accordingly.
