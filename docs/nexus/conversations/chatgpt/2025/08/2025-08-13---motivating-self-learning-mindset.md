> Here’s a no-BS, Obsidian-ready handout you can give him. It’s built to kill the “I’m stupid” loop and replace it with repeatable habits.
> 
> # The Beginner’s Edge: A practical playbook for learning to code
> 
> > “In the beginner’s mind there are many possibilities; in the expert’s there are few.” 
> > “The master has failed more times than the beginner has even tried.” 
> > “Shoshaku jushaku” — one mistake after another. 
> > “The obstacle is the way.”
> 
> ## 0) Ground rules (read this first)
> - You’re not “stupid.” You’re **new**. New ≠ bad; it’s a stage. 
> - Progress = **reps × focus**, not talent. Ship small things often. 
> - Replace apologies with **status + next step** (script below). 
> - Boredom and confusion are **signals**, not verdicts.
> 
> ## 1) Anti-apology scripts (use verbatim)
> - Instead of “Sorry I’m dumb”: 
>  → “I’m new at _X_. Blocker: _Y_. I tried _A, B_. Next: _Z_. Can you sanity-check my next step?”
> - Instead of “Sorry I’m slow”: 
>  → “This is hard **and worth it**. I’m taking one more micro-step: _Z_. I’ll report back.”
> - Instead of “I don’t get it”: 
>  → “I’m stuck at step _N_. Expected _E_, got _R_. I suspect _H_. I’ll test _T_ next.”
> 
> ## 2) Session recipe (45–60 min)
> 1. **Define outcome (2 min):** one sentence: “By the end I want _X_ working.”
> 2. **Warm-up rep (5 min):** retype a tiny example from docs/tests. Don’t “understand”—just run it.
> 3. **Deep work (25 min):** one problem, no tabs, phone away. 
> 4. **Fast review (5 min):** write the “3-line log” (below). 
> 5. **Optional second block (25 min):** only if you still have fuel.
> 
> **3-line log template** 
> - What I tried → 
> - What happened → 
> - What I’ll try next →
> 
> ## 3) Stuck protocol (15-minute ladder)
> If you’re spinning, climb this ladder in order. Don’t skip rungs.
> 1. **State the intent:** “I’m trying to _X_.” 
> 2. **Minify:** isolate the smallest input that fails (make a tiny file/test). 
> 3. **Instrument:** add one `print` / log / assertion where you expect truth to hold. 
> 4. **Swap the variable:** change one thing only (data, path, flag). 
> 5. **Read the error literally:** first unfamiliar word → look it up. 
> 6. **Search with nouns + exact text:** “Node ERR_MODULE_NOT_FOUND import path resolution”. 
> 7. **Rubber duck:** explain it out loud or in your log. 
> 8. **Ask for help:** use the anti-apology script with your 3-line log.
> 
> ## 4) What to measure (and what to ignore)
> Measure:
> - Minutes of focused practice per day (aim 30–90). 
> - Reps: number of **tiny projects** or tests shipped per week (aim 3–5). 
> - “Stucks resolved” per week (aim 5+). 
> - Notes created: one distilled note per session.
> 
> Ignore:
> - Lines of code. 
> - How many languages you “know.” 
> - Comparing day 10 to someone else’s year 3.
> 
> ## 5) Micro-wins to build momentum
> Pick one per day; ship it.
> - CLI: number-guessing game; extend with “lives,” then a scoreboard file. 
> - Web: single HTML page that fetches a public JSON API and lists items; add a search box. 
> - Tests: write 3 tests for a function you didn’t write. Make one fail on purpose. 
> - I/O: load a CSV, compute one stat, and write a new CSV.
> 
> ## 6) How to ask for help (mentor-ready format)
> Paste this when you message your mentor:
> ```
> Goal: <one sentence>
> Context: <repo/file/branch or minimal snippet>
> What I tried: <A, B>
> Observed: <exact error/output>
> Hypothesis: <best guess>
> Next step: <one thing you’ll do after this message>
> ```
> 
> ## 7) Mindset: keep the beginner’s edge without the shame
> - You chose a **hard** path on purpose. That’s intelligence, not failure. 
> - Your brain evolved for social survival, not compilers. Feeling “wrong” is expected. 
> - Treat mistakes as **samples**—data you collect, not verdicts about your ability. 
> - If you catch “I’m dumb,” run this if-then: 
>  **If** I think “I’m dumb,” **then** I write the 3-line log and take one next step.
> 
> ## 8) Weekly cadence (minimum viable plan)
> - **3× Focus blocks (60–90 min):** learn/build. 
> - **1× Shipping hour:** polish and publish one tiny thing (repo or gist). 
> - **1× Review (30 min):** read your week’s logs; write 5 bullets: wins, stucks, themes, one rule you’ll try next week, one question for your mentor.
> 
> ## 9) Debug checklist (pin this)
> - [ ] Can I reproduce it in a **tiny** file? 
> - [ ] Do I have **one** failing test? 
> - [ ] Do I know **where** it goes wrong (a log/assert proves it)? 
> - [ ] Did I change **one** thing at a time? 
> - [ ] Do I understand **one** new term from the error? 
> - [ ] Did I write the 3-line log?
> 
> ## 10) Agreements (so this actually works)
> **Your part**
> - Show up, even tired, for one micro-win. 
> - Log each session with the 3-line template. 
> - Ask for help in the mentor format.
> 
> **Mentor’s part**
> - I’ll challenge your thinking, not your worth. 
> - I’ll respond to your logs with the smallest next experiment. 
> - I’ll praise **effort quality** (clear logs, tiny tests), not natural talent.
> 
> ---
> 
> If you keep this loop going, the “I’m stupid” narration will get crowded out by data. You’ll still feel dumb some days. That’s fine. Ship anyway.
